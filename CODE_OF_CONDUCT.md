# Code of Conduct

Adapted from the [Contributor Covenant v2.1](https://www.contributor-covenant.org/version/2/1/code_of_conduct/), scoped for a university capstone team.

## Our Standard

As a team, we commit to:
- Treating every teammate's contribution — code, documentation, or project management — as equally valuable to the final grade and product.
- Giving feedback on work, not on people. Critique the PR, not the person.
- Being explicit about availability and blockers rather than going silent — this is a 3-person team and one unresponsive member stalls the whole project.
- Crediting all contributors accurately in commits, PRs, and the final report.

## Unacceptable Behaviour

- Dismissing a teammate's input because they are less technical.
- Merging work without review to save time.
- Taking credit in the final report for work done by another member.
- Missing agreed deadlines without communicating in advance.

## Scope

Applies to all project spaces: this repository, team meetings, shared documents, and any project-related communication channel.

## Enforcement

Concerns are raised directly with the team first. If unresolved, escalate to the unit coordinator, consistent with CQUniversity's academic misconduct and group-work policies.
